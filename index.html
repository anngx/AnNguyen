A Bit About Me:

I am currently a 4th year Studio Arts and Digital Narrative & Interactive Design student. This semester, I’m taking 6 courses total including Creative Coding and Painting 3. For my painting project series, I am creating a body of work centered around queerness and the notion of radical self-love.
When I first entered university, I was set on pursuing Computational Biology. Although I passed all of my examinations that first semester, I was completely miserable and felt no passion for my studies. During my second semester at Pitt, I took 1 drawing course and immediately fell in love, with everything from the course work to the intimate class sizes. However, I soon realized that exclusively pursuing Studio Arts is impractical and frankly not worth it considering Pitt’s tuition. I looked into lucrative creative career options and began independently studying UX Design. I was a junior when Pitt came out with their DNID major, for which I discovered I already had a decent amount of credits from previously taking CS courses, so I went for it.



Family: Chosen & Biological

I was born in Ho Chi Minh City, Vietnam. In 2004, my family immigrated to the United States, and we eventually settled in Pennsylvania. Having always supported and motivated me to be the best version of myself, my father contributes largely to the person I am today. Aside from my family, my friends, partner, and rescued dog Barnie also play a big role in my life.









How I Arrived at UX:

My passions include Art, Design, and altogether making the world a better place. UX Design and Usability Testing neatly combine these three things, as it allows me to express my creative passions as well as help people move through life more fluidly. Being someone who lives with physical handicaps, it allows me to directly wear the shoes of those who would most benefit from usability testing.







Plans for the Future:

After school, I plan on continuing furthering my design career and working where I currently intern, doing visual design work and marketing. Eventually, I would like to venture into business and become self-employed.
